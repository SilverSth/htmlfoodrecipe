Recipe for the Chatamari.

Chatamari, also known as Nepali pizza, is a traditional Nepali dish that is made with a rice flour base and topped with a variety of savory ingredients. It is a popular street food in Nepal and is often served as a snack or a light meal. Chatamari is a delicious and versatile dish that can be customized to suit individual preferences. Whether you prefer a meat or vegetarian version, spicy or mild, chatamari is a satisfying and flavorful Nepali dish that is sure to please your taste buds.

Ingredient for the recipe.

For the base:

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1/2 cups water
  • Salt to taste

For the toppings:

  • 1/2 cup minced meat (chicken, lamb or buffalo) or vegetables (mushrooms, onions, bell peppers, etc.)
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro leaves
  • 1/4 cup grated cheese
  • 1 egg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for cooking

For the tomato sauce:

  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin

Instruction for the recipe

For tomato sauce

  1. In a saucepan, heat oil over medium heat. Add chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
  2. Add minced garlic and sauté for a minute.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes, red chili powder, ground cumin, and salt. Mix well.
  4. Cover the saucepan and cook for 5-7 minutes, until the tomatoes are soft and cooked.
  5. Transfer the tomato mixture to a blender and blend until smooth.

For chatamari

  1. In a mixing bowl, add rice flour, water, and salt. Mix well to form a smooth batter.
  2. Heat a non-stick pan or a griddle over medium heat. Grease it with some oil.
  3. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the pan and spread it evenly in a circular shape to form a thin base.
  4. Cook for 3-4 minutes until the base is firm and slightly golden.
  5. Now in a small mixing bowl, add minced meat or vegetables, cilantro leaves, grated cheese, egg, salt, and pepper. Mix well to combine.
  6. Spread the topping mixture evenly over the rice flour base.
  7. Cover the pan and cook for 5-7 minutes over medium heat, until the toppings are cooked and slightly melted.
  8. Now cut the chatamari into slices or wedges.
  9. Drizzle the tomato sauce over the chatamari.
  10. Garnish with chopped cilantro leaves.
  11. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious Nepali pizza, chatamari!

Now enjoy your delicious Nepali pizza, chatamari!
